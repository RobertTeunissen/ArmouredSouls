## Overview

Every robot in Armoured Souls uses one of four **loadout types** that determines how weapons are equipped and what combat bonuses (and penalties) apply. Your loadout choice is one of the most impactful strategic decisions you'll make — it shapes your robot's identity as a fighter.

Loadout bonuses are **percentage-based**, which means they scale with your robot's attributes as you level up. A +20% bonus at level 5 is nice; at level 40, it's transformative. This keeps loadout choice meaningful throughout your entire progression.

## The Four Loadout Types

### 🗡️ Single Weapon

**Equipment:** One weapon in the main hand, offhand empty.

The Single loadout is the most straightforward configuration — one weapon, no complications. It provides modest bonuses with zero penalties, making it the safest and most flexible option.

| Bonus | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Gyro Stabilizers** | +10% — Improves your robot's balance and reaction time, reducing incoming hit chance |
| **Servo Motors** | +5% — Enhances mobility and positioning |

**Penalties:** None.

**Compatible Weapons:** All 22 one-handed weapons (Energy, Ballistic, and Melee categories).

```callout-tip
Single loadout is often underestimated. The absence of penalties means every attribute point you invest works at full value. Combined with the Gyro Stabilizers bonus, Single-loadout robots are surprisingly hard to hit — making this a strong choice for balanced or evasion-focused builds.
```

**Best For:**
- New players learning the game
- Balanced builds that don't want to commit to a specialization
- Evasion-focused robots that rely on not getting hit
- Budget builds where you only want to purchase one weapon

---

### 🛡️ Weapon + Shield

**Equipment:** One weapon in the main hand, one shield in the offhand.

The Weapon+Shield loadout is the defensive powerhouse. It provides the strongest defensive bonuses of any loadout at the cost of attack speed. This is the configuration for robots that want to outlast their opponents.

| Bonus | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Shield Capacity** | +20% — Significantly larger energy shield pool to absorb incoming damage |
| **Armor Plating** | +15% — More damage reduction on every hit that reaches your hull |
| **Counter Protocols** | +10% — Higher chance to counter-attack when targeted |

| Penalty | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Attack Speed** | -15% — Slower attacks due to the weight of carrying a shield |

**Compatible Weapons:** Any one-handed weapon in the main slot + any shield (Light Shield, Combat Shield, Reactive Shield, Barrier Shield, Fortress Shield, or Aegis Bulwark) in the offhand slot. Your main weapon must be equipped before you can equip a shield.

```callout-info
The -15% Attack Speed penalty sounds harsh, but defensive builds often don't mind. If your strategy is to absorb damage and counter-attack, you're winning through durability rather than speed. The +10% Counter Protocols bonus partially compensates by giving you free bonus attacks.
```

**Best For:**
- Tank builds focused on survivability
- Counter-attack strategies (pair with high Counter Protocols attribute)
- Players who prefer attrition warfare — winning by outlasting the opponent
- Robots in higher league tiers where battles are longer and durability matters more

---

### ⚔️ Two-Handed

**Equipment:** One two-handed weapon occupying both slots.

The Two-Handed loadout is built for maximum burst damage. It provides offensive bonuses including a direct damage multiplier and an enhanced critical hit multiplier, making every hit count. The trade-off is reduced evasion — you're committing to hitting hard rather than dodging.

| Bonus | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Combat Power** | +10% — More raw damage on every attack |
| **Critical Systems** | +20% — Significantly higher critical hit chance |
| **Damage Multiplier** | 1.10× — A flat 10% damage increase applied during damage calculation |
| **Critical Multiplier** | 2.5× — Enhanced critical hit damage (higher than the standard multiplier) |

| Penalty | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Evasion Thrusters** | -10% — Reduced ability to dodge incoming attacks |

**Compatible Weapons:** All 19 two-handed weapons — Training Rifle, Training Beam, Shotgun, Grenade Launcher, Sniper Rifle, Battle Axe, Plasma Cannon, Heavy Hammer, Railgun, Ion Beam, War Club, Shock Maul, Thermal Lance, Laser Rifle, Scatter Cannon, Pulse Accelerator, Arc Projector, Mortar System, and Siege Cannon.

```callout-tip
The combination of +20% Critical Systems and the 2.5× critical multiplier makes Two-Handed the undisputed king of burst damage. When a critical hit lands with a two-handed weapon, the damage spike can be devastating. Pair this with Offensive stance for maximum impact.
```

**Best For:**
- Glass cannon builds that prioritize damage above all else
- Critical hit-focused strategies
- Players who want decisive, quick battles
- Robots with high Combat Power and Critical Systems attributes

---

### 🔄 Dual-Wield

**Equipment:** Two one-handed weapons, one in each hand.

Dual-Wield is the sustained DPS loadout. Two weapons means two independent attack cycles, giving you roughly double the attack frequency. The bonuses amplify speed and reliability, while the penalties reduce per-hit damage. It's about volume over power.

| Bonus | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **Attack Speed** | +30% — Dramatically faster attacks from both weapons |
| **Weapon Control** | +15% — Better weapon reliability, fewer malfunctions |

| Penalty | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| **Penetration** | -20% — Reduced ability to bypass armor |
| **Combat Power** | -10% — Each individual hit deals less damage |

**Compatible Weapons:** All 22 one-handed weapons in both slots. You can equip two copies of the same weapon or mix different weapons for asymmetric builds.

```callout-info
Dual-Wield's offhand weapon has significant combat penalties — 50% base hit chance (vs 70% for main hand) and a 40% cooldown penalty. These are fundamental to the loadout's balance. See [Dual-Wield Mechanics](/guide/weapons/dual-wield-mechanics) for the full breakdown of how per-hand bonuses and offhand rules work.
```

**Best For:**
- Speed-focused builds that overwhelm through attack frequency
- Sustained DPS strategies where consistent damage output matters more than burst
- Players who enjoy optimizing two weapons with complementary bonuses
- Robots with high Attack Speed and Weapon Control attributes

## Weapon Compatibility at a Glance

Not every weapon works with every loadout. Here's the compatibility matrix:

| Weapon Type | Single | Weapon+Shield | Two-Handed | Dual-Wield |
|-------------|--------|---------------|------------|------------|
| One-handed weapons (22) | ✅ Main | ✅ Main only | ❌ | ✅ Both slots |
| Two-handed weapons (19) | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ Main (both hands) | ❌ |
| Shield weapons (6) | ❌ | ✅ Offhand only | ❌ | ❌ |

```callout-tip
You can change your loadout type at any time from the robot detail page. However, if your currently equipped weapons aren't compatible with the new loadout, you'll need to unequip them first. Plan your weapon purchases with your target loadout in mind.
```

## How Loadout Bonuses Work
## Weapon Ownership Rules

A few important rules about how weapons work across your stable:

- Each weapon you own can only be **equipped on one robot at a time**. If you want two robots using the same weapon type, you need to buy two copies.
- Every weapon in your inventory takes up a slot in your **Storage Facility**. Upgrading your storage lets you own more weapons. Plan your purchases around your available capacity.
- You can **swap weapons between robots freely** at any time outside of battle. There's no cost or cooldown — just unequip from one robot and equip on another. This lets you experiment with different loadouts without buying duplicate weapons.

## How Loadout Bonuses Work
Loadout bonuses are **percentage-based multipliers** applied to your robot's effective attributes (base + weapon bonuses). The formula works like this:

> **Effective Attribute = (Base Attribute + Weapon Bonuses) × (1 + Loadout Bonus %)**

This means loadout bonuses become more powerful as your attributes grow. A +20% Shield Capacity bonus on a robot with 10 Shield Capacity adds 2 effective points. On a robot with 40 Shield Capacity, it adds 8. This is why loadout choice remains strategically important at every stage of the game.

## Weapon Range Bands

Every weapon has an **optimal range band** that determines where it deals the most damage. Fighting at the right distance is critical — see [Movement & Positioning](/guide/combat/movement-and-positioning) for the full breakdown.

| Optimal Range | Weapons | Distance |
|--------------|---------|----------|
| **Melee** (0–2 units) | All melee weapons (Energy Blade, Vibro Sword, Power Fist, Chain Sword, Thermal Lance, Vibro Mace, War Club, Shock Maul), all shields | Cannot attack beyond 2 units |
| **Short** (3–6 units) | One-handed ranged (Laser Pistol, Plasma Pistol, Machine Pistol, Pulse Rifle, Ion Pistol, Volt Sabre), two-handed short (Scatter Cannon, Laser Rifle, Pulse Accelerator, Arc Projector) | Best for Single and Dual-Wield |
| **Mid** (7–12 units) | One-handed mid (Bolt Carbine, Flux Repeater, Disruptor Cannon, Nova Caster), two-handed mid (Shotgun, Grenade Launcher, Plasma Cannon, Mortar System) | Best for varied loadouts |
| **Long** (13+ units) | One-handed long (Beam Pistol, Photon Marksman, Gauss Pistol, Particle Lance), two-handed long (Sniper Rifle, Railgun, Ion Beam, Siege Cannon) | Best for Two-Handed loadout |

Range penalties apply when fighting outside your weapon's optimal band:

| Situation | Damage Multiplier |
|-----------|------------------|
| At optimal range | **1.1×** (+10% bonus) |
| One band away | **0.75×** (-25%) |
| Two+ bands away | **0.5×** (-50%) |

```callout-tip
Your loadout choice and weapon range should align. A Dual-Wield build with two melee weapons needs high Servo Motors to close the gap. A Two-Handed Sniper Rifle build wants to maintain long range. Mismatched range strategy is one of the most common mistakes — a robot with a long-range weapon that keeps getting caught in melee range is fighting at half damage.
```

## Matching Loadout to Strategy

| Strategy | Recommended Loadout | Why |
|----------|-------------------|-----|
| Maximum survivability | Weapon+Shield | Triple defensive bonuses stack with defensive attributes |
| Burst damage / one-shot potential | Two-Handed | Damage multiplier + crit bonuses for devastating spikes |
| Sustained DPS / speed | Dual-Wield | Two attack cycles + 30% Attack Speed bonus |
| Balanced / flexible | Single | No penalties, modest bonuses, works with any attribute spread |
| Counter-attack focused | Weapon+Shield | +10% Counter Protocols + Defensive stance synergy |
| Critical hit focused | Two-Handed | +20% Critical Systems + 2.5× crit multiplier |

## Loadout Considerations for King of the Hill

In KotH's 5-6 robot free-for-all, loadout choice takes on new dimensions. **Weapon+Shield** builds excel at holding the Control Zone under pressure from multiple attackers, while **Two-Handed** sniper builds can rack up kill bonuses (5 points each) from outside the zone. **Dual-Wield** builds benefit from sustained DPS to quickly clear contested zones. Consider survivability more heavily than in 1v1 — being eliminated early in a KotH match means a poor placement and minimal rewards. See the [KotH Strategy](/guide/king-of-the-hill/koth-strategy) guide for detailed build recommendations.
