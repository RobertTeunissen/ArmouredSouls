## Overview

One of the first strategic decisions you'll make in Armoured Souls is how many robots to run. Your starting budget of ₡3,000,000 can support one, two, or three robots — each approach has distinct advantages and drawbacks.

There's no objectively "best" choice. It depends on whether you prefer quality over quantity, how much risk you're comfortable with, and how you want your income to scale over time.

## The Three Approaches

### 🤖 One Mighty Robot

Put all your resources into a single powerhouse.

**Budget breakdown:**

| Category | Amount | Details |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Facilities | ₡300,000 | Weapons Workshop, Training Facility, Repair Bay |
| Robots | ₡500,000 | 1 robot frame |
| Weapons | ₡550,000 | 1–2 quality weapons for your chosen loadout |
| Attributes | ₡1,550,000 | Heavy investment in key attributes |
| Reserve | ₡50,000 | Covers immediate repairs and operating costs |

**Strengths:**
- Highest individual attribute levels from day one
- Dominates opponents at the same league tier
- Faster promotion through leagues means higher battle rewards sooner
- Simplest to manage — one robot, one strategy to optimize
- Large reserve fund for facilities and future upgrades

**Weaknesses:**
- Only one battle per cycle means less total income
- Single point of failure — if your robot has a bad matchup, you earn nothing that cycle
- Less streaming revenue (fewer battles for the Streaming Studio to multiply)
- Slower Fame accumulation (only one robot earning Fame)

```callout-tip
The one-robot approach is great for players who enjoy deep optimization. You can pour every credit into making one robot the best it can be.
```

### 🤖🤖 Two Average Robots

Split your resources between two competent fighters.

**Budget breakdown:**

| Category | Amount | Details |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Facilities | ₡350,000 | Roster Expansion L1 + discount facilities |
| Robots | ₡1,000,000 | 2 robot frames |
| Weapons | ₡500,000 | 2–4 weapons across both robots |
| Attributes | ₡1,100,000 | Split between both robots |
| Reserve | ₡50,000 | Covers immediate repairs and operating costs |

**Strengths:**
- Two battles per cycle — double the income opportunities
- Risk is spread across two robots (one bad matchup doesn't wipe your cycle)
- Can experiment with different builds (one offensive, one defensive)
- More streaming revenue from additional battles
- Two robots earning Fame independently

**Weaknesses:**
- Each robot is weaker individually than a single focused robot
- More complex to manage — two sets of upgrades, weapons, and configurations
- Higher total repair costs after each cycle
- Smaller reserve fund for facilities

```callout-info
Two robots is the most popular starting choice. It balances income generation with competitive strength and gives you room to experiment with different strategies.
```

### 🤖🤖🤖 Three Flimsy Robots

Maximize your battle count with three budget fighters.

**Budget breakdown:**

| Category | Amount | Details |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Facilities | ₡350,000 | Roster Expansion L2 + discount facilities |
| Robots | ₡1,500,000 | 3 robot frames |
| Weapons | ₡450,000 | 3–6 weapons, at least one per robot |
| Attributes | ₡650,000 | Spread thin across all three robots |
| Reserve | ₡50,000 | Covers immediate repairs and operating costs |

**Strengths:**
- Three battles per cycle — maximum income potential at Bronze tier
- Best streaming revenue scaling (more battles = more streaming income)
- Three robots earning Fame means faster aggregate Fame growth
- If one robot loses, the other two can still earn
- Most flexibility for experimenting with different builds

**Weaknesses:**
- Each robot starts significantly weaker
- Higher total operating and repair costs
- Smallest reserve fund — less room for error
- Slower individual progression through leagues
- More management overhead — three robots to configure and upgrade

```callout-warning
Three robots stretches your starting budget thin. Each robot will be noticeably weaker in early battles. This approach rewards patience — it pays off as income from three sources compounds over time.
```

## Side-by-Side Comparison

| Factor | 1 Robot | 2 Robots | 3 Robots |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| Starting attribute levels | Highest | Medium | Lowest |
| Battles per cycle | 1 | 2 | 3 |
| Income per cycle | Low (1 source) | Medium (2 sources) | High (3 sources) |
| Repair costs per cycle | Low | Medium | High |
| Streaming revenue | Low | Medium | Highest |
| Risk of zero-income cycle | Higher | Lower | Lowest |
| Management complexity | Simple | Moderate | Complex |
| League promotion speed | Fastest | Medium | Slowest |
| Reserve fund | Largest | Medium | Smallest |
| Early game strength | Strongest | Balanced | Weakest |
| Late game income | Lower | Balanced | Highest |

## Which Should You Choose?

**Choose 1 robot if you:**
- Want to climb leagues quickly
- Prefer simplicity and deep optimization
- Like having a large financial cushion
- Don't mind slower overall income growth

**Choose 2 robots if you:**
- Want a balance of strength and income
- Like experimenting with different builds
- Want some insurance against bad matchups
- Prefer a moderate management workload

**Choose 3 robots if you:**
- Want to maximize long-term income
- Enjoy managing multiple strategies
- Are comfortable with weaker early performance
- Plan to invest in the Streaming Studio early for multiplied revenue

## Changing Your Mind Later

Your initial choice isn't permanent. As you earn credits, you can always buy additional robots through the Roster Expansion facility (up to 10 robots total). Many players start with one or two robots and expand their roster as their income grows.

The Roster Expansion facility costs ₡150,000 for the first additional slot, with costs increasing for each subsequent slot. Each extra robot slot also adds ₡500/day in operating costs.

```callout-tip
A common progression path: start with 2 robots, build up your income and facilities over 10–20 cycles, then expand to 3 or more robots once your economy is stable.
```
