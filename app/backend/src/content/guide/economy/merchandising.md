## Overview

Merchandising is a **passive income stream** tied to your stable's **Prestige**. As your stable builds a reputation through league competition and tournament success, fans start buying branded merchandise — robot figurines, team jerseys, posters, and more. You don't manage the merchandise directly; the income flows automatically based on how prestigious your stable has become.

Merchandising starts small but scales meaningfully as your Prestige grows. For established stables, it becomes a reliable supplement to battle winnings that helps smooth out the financial ups and downs of competitive play.

## How Merchandising Works

Merchandising income is calculated once per cycle during the daily processing. It requires the **Merchandising Hub** facility — without it, you earn zero merchandising income regardless of your Prestige.

### The Merchandising Hub

The Merchandising Hub is the foundation of your merchandising income. Each level sets a higher base income rate, and your Prestige then multiplies that base. No Hub means no merchandise sales.

- **Level 1** — Unlocks merchandising income at a modest base rate
- **Higher levels** — Each upgrade increases the base rate, which your Prestige then amplifies

The Hub has a daily operating cost, so it only makes financial sense once your Prestige is high enough to generate income that exceeds the facility's upkeep.

```callout-info
The Merchandising Hub is a mid-to-late game investment. Don't rush to build it — wait until your Prestige has grown enough for the income to justify the operating costs.
```

### Prestige as a Multiplier

Your stable's Prestige acts as a continuous multiplier on your merchandising base rate. Every point of Prestige you earn increases your merchandising income smoothly — there are no sudden jumps at rank boundaries. But to give you a sense of how it scales across the ranks:

| Prestige Rank | Prestige Required | Merchandising Impact |
|---------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Novice** | 0 | Negligible — your stable is unknown, so there's almost no demand for merchandise |
| **Established** | 1,000 | Small but noticeable — a modest fanbase starts generating some passive income |
| **Veteran** | 5,000 | Meaningful — merchandising becomes a reliable secondary income stream |
| **Elite** | 10,000 | Significant — your brand is well-known and merchandise sells consistently |
| **Champion** | 25,000 | Substantial — a large, loyal fanbase drives strong merchandise revenue |
| **Legendary** | 50,000 | Maximum — your stable is iconic, and merchandise income is a major part of your finances |

```callout-tip
Prestige grows naturally as your robots compete in higher league tiers. You don't need to do anything special to earn it — just keep fighting and promoting. The merchandising income follows automatically.
```

## When Merchandising Pays Off

Merchandising income is most valuable in two scenarios:

### Steady Income During Bad Cycles

Battle winnings fluctuate — your robots might lose several battles in a row, or take heavy damage that eats into profits through repair costs. Merchandising income is consistent regardless of battle outcomes. It provides a financial floor that keeps your stable afloat during rough patches.

### Scaling with Prestige

As your Prestige grows, merchandising becomes a genuinely significant income source. A high-Prestige stable with a maxed Merchandising Hub earns enough from merchandise alone to cover a substantial portion of daily operating costs — effectively making your facilities cheaper to run.

## Merchandising vs Other Income

| Income Source | Depends On | Consistency | Scaling |
|---------------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Battle Winnings | League tier, win/loss, Prestige | Variable per cycle | Scales with tier promotion |
| Merchandising | Prestige, Merchandising Hub | Very consistent | Scales with Prestige |
| Streaming | Fame, battles fought, Streaming Studio | Moderate consistency | Scales with Fame and activity |

Merchandising is the most predictable of the three income sources. It doesn't spike or dip based on battle results — it's a steady drip that rewards long-term reputation building.

```callout-tip
Think of merchandising as your stable's "salary" — it shows up every cycle regardless of how battles went. Battle winnings are your "bonus" — variable and performance-dependent. A healthy stable has both.
```
