## Overview

**Achievements** are one-time milestones you earn by playing the game. Every achievement awards **Credits** and **Prestige**, plus a collectible hexagonal **badge** that you can display on your stable page. They give you concrete goals to work toward and celebrate your accomplishments along the way.

Achievements cover everything — winning battles, reaching league tiers, building your stable's economy, hitting prestige and fame thresholds, and pulling off specific combat feats. Some are easy to earn in your first session; others take months of dedicated play.

```callout-info
Achievements are purely celebratory. They award Credits and Prestige but never grant combat bonuses, unlock gameplay mechanics, or gate access to features. They're trophies, not power-ups.
```

## Difficulty Tiers

Every achievement belongs to one of five difficulty tiers. The tier determines the reward amount and the badge border color.

| Tier | Credits | Prestige | Badge Border | When You'll Earn Them |
|------|---------|----------|-------------|----------------------|
| 🟢 **Easy** | ₡25,000 | +25 | Green | First week of play |
| 🔵 **Medium** | ₡50,000 | +50 | Blue | Weeks 2–6 |
| 🟠 **Hard** | ₡100,000 | +100 | Orange | Months of play |
| 🔴 **Very Hard** | ₡250,000 | +250 | Red | Multiple seasons |
| ⬛ **Secret** | ₡50,000 | +50 | Purple | Hidden until earned |

The full set of achievements is deliberately **not completable in a single season**. The Very Hard tier alone requires sustained play over multiple seasons. Completionists will always have something to chase.

```callout-tip
Don't worry about completing everything. Easy achievements give you quick wins early on, while Hard and Very Hard achievements are long-term goals that reward dedication over time.
```

## How Achievements Are Earned

Achievements are evaluated **automatically** after game actions. You don't need to claim them or check a box — when you meet the condition, the achievement unlocks instantly.

### After Battles

Most achievements are checked after a battle completes. This includes win counts, kill counts, ELO thresholds, win streaks, and special combat feats like perfect victories or low-HP comebacks.

### After Other Actions

Some achievements trigger from non-combat actions:

- **Weapon purchases and equips** — Buying your first weapon, owning 10 weapons, equipping specific weapon types
- **Attribute upgrades** — Upgrading a robot for the first time, reaching high effective stats
- **Facility upgrades** — Owning multiple high-level facilities
- **Robot creation** — Growing your roster to 3, 5, or 7 robots
- **Tuning allocation** — Using the Tuning Bay for the first time, maxing out tuning points
- **League promotions** — Reaching Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, or Champion league
- **Tournament wins** — Winning your first tournament, winning multiple tournaments

### Prestige & Fame Milestones

Achievements also fire when your stable hits prestige thresholds (1,000 / 5,000 / 10,000 / 25,000) or when a robot reaches fame tiers (Known / Famous / Legendary / Mythical). These are the achievements that turn the [Prestige](/guide/prestige-fame/prestige-system) and [Fame](/guide/prestige-fame/fame-system) systems from silent number-ticking into celebrated moments.

## Tracking Your Progress

Open the **Achievements** page from the main navigation to see every achievement, your progress, and what you've earned.

### Progress Bars

Every achievement with a numeric target shows a progress bar with exact values. For example, an achievement requiring 50 wins might show "34 / 50 wins" with a colored progress bar. The bar color matches the achievement's tier.

For **robot-level achievements** (like win counts or ELO thresholds), the progress shows your best robot's value. If you have three robots with 12, 34, and 8 wins, a "50 wins" achievement shows "34 / 50 wins (best: RobotName)".

### Secret Achievements

Secret achievements are hidden until you earn them. On the achievements page, they appear as "???" with a purple placeholder badge and no progress information. Once earned, they reveal their name, description, and badge like any other achievement.

```callout-info
Secret achievements are triggered by unusual or unexpected gameplay moments. You won't know what they are until you stumble into them — that's the fun.
```

## Rarity

Every achievement shows how many players have earned it, giving you a sense of how rare your accomplishments are.

| Rarity Label | % of Players | Color |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **Common** | > 75% | Grey |
| **Uncommon** | 25–75% | Green |
| **Rare** | 10–25% | Blue |
| **Epic** | 1–10% | Amber |
| **Legendary** | < 1% | Red |

Rarity is updated once per game cycle, not in real-time. It's based on the number of active players (anyone who has fought at least one battle).

## Toast Notifications

When you earn an achievement, a toast notification slides in from the top-right of the screen showing the badge, achievement name, reward summary, and rarity label. If you earn multiple achievements at once (common after a big battle), they stack. Click a toast to jump to that achievement on the achievements page.
