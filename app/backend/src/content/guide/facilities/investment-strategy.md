## Overview

With 13 facilities to choose from and a limited budget, knowing what to buy and when is critical. This guide provides a recommended investment roadmap for each stage of the game, based on ROI analysis and strategic value.

![Investment priority roadmap](/images/guide/facilities/investment-priority-roadmap.png)

The key principle: **invest in facilities that either save you money or make you money before investing in facilities that provide power**. A facility that pays for itself in 30 cycles is better than one that makes your robots 5% stronger but drains your balance.

You can track your facility investment performance on the **Investment Overview** tab of the Facilities page. This tab shows lifetime ROI data for the 5 economic facilities (Merchandising Hub, Streaming Studio, Repair Bay, Training Facility, Weapons Workshop) including total investment, total returns, whether each facility has paid for itself, and projected payoff cycles if not yet profitable. The data is sourced from cycle snapshots for accuracy, with formula-based estimates as a fallback when snapshot data is unavailable.

## Early Game (Cycles 1–50)

**Budget**: ₡3,000,000 starting balance, Bronze/Silver league income

Your priority is stretching your starting budget and building a foundation for growth. Every Credit counts.

### Priority 1: Training Facility (Level 1–3)

- **Cost**: ₡150,000 (Level 1)
- **Why first**: 10% discount on all attribute upgrades. You'll be upgrading constantly in the early game, so this pays for itself almost immediately.
- **Target**: Level 3 (₡900,000 total, 30% discount) by cycle 30

### Priority 2: Repair Bay (Level 1–2)

- **Cost**: ₡50,000 (Level 1)
- **Why second**: Repair costs are your biggest variable expense. Even a small discount saves meaningful Credits every cycle.
- **Target**: Level 2 (₡300,000 total) by cycle 20

### Priority 3: One Training Academy (Level 1–2)

- **Cost**: ₡100,000 (Level 1)
- **Why third**: Unlocking attribute caps from 10 to 15–20 is a significant power boost that helps you compete in Silver league.
- **Which one**: Match your primary robot's build. Combat Academy for damage dealers, Defense Academy for tanks.

```callout-tip
In the early game, resist the urge to buy everything. Three facilities at low levels (Training Facility, Repair Bay, one Academy) cost about ₡350,000 in upgrades plus ~₡500/day in operating costs. That's manageable on Bronze/Silver income. Adding more facilities too early can drain your budget.
```

### Early Game Budget Allocation

| Category | Allocation | Amount |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Robot purchase | 30–40% | ₡900K–₡1.2M |
| Weapons | 15–20% | ₡450K–₡600K |
| Attribute upgrades | 20–25% | ₡600K–₡750K |
| Facilities | 15–20% | ₡450K–₡600K |
| Reserve | 5–10% | ₡150K–₡300K |

## Mid Game (Cycles 50–150)

**Budget**: Gold/Platinum league income, growing Prestige

Your income is now stable enough to support more facilities. Focus on income-generating facilities and expanding your roster.

### Priority 4: Streaming Studio (Level 1–3)

- **Cost**: ₡100,000 (Level 1)
- **Why now**: Lowest operating cost of any facility (₡100/day). By mid game, your robots have enough Fame to make streaming revenue meaningful. The 2×–4× multiplier on streaming income is excellent ROI.

### Priority 5: Roster Expansion (Level 1–3)

- **Cost**: ₡150,000 (Level 1)
- **Why now**: More robots means more battles, more income streams, and better Repair Bay scaling. Going from 1 to 3–4 robots roughly triples your battle income.

### Priority 6: Second Training Academy (Level 1–3)

- **Cost**: ₡100,000 per level
- **Why now**: Your secondary attributes are hitting the cap. A second academy lets you develop more well-rounded robots.

### Priority 7: Merchandising Hub (Level 1–3)

- **Cost**: ₡150,000 (Level 1)
- **Why now**: By mid game, your Prestige should be at Established or Veteran rank. The Merchandising Hub amplifies your Prestige into consistent passive income. At 5,000 Prestige with Level 3, you earn ₡22,500/day against ₡600/day operating cost.

```callout-info
Mid game is when your facility portfolio starts generating real returns. The Streaming Studio and Merchandising Hub together can cover a significant portion of your daily operating costs, creating a financial safety net.
```

### Mid Game Facility Targets

| Facility | Target Level | Total Investment | Daily Operating Cost |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Training Facility | Level 5 | ₡2,250,000 | ₡1,250 |
| Repair Bay | Level 4 | ₡500,000 | ₡400 |
| Primary Academy | Level 4–5 | ₡1,000,000–₡1,500,000 | ₡1,000–₡1,250 |
| Secondary Academy | Level 2–3 | ₡300,000–₡600,000 | ₡500–₡750 |
| Streaming Studio | Level 3 | ₡600,000 | ₡300 |
| Roster Expansion | Level 3 | ₡900,000 | ₡1,500 |
| Merchandising Hub | Level 3 | ₡900,000 | ₡600 |

**Total daily operating cost**: ~₡7,650/day — comfortably covered by Gold/Platinum income with 4 robots.

## Late Game (Cycles 150+)

**Budget**: Diamond/Champion league income, high Prestige and Fame

Income is no longer a constraint. Focus on maximizing power and unlocking endgame content.

### Priority 8: Push Academies to Level 7–10

- **Why**: Attribute caps of 42–50 are essential for competing in Diamond and Champion leagues. The difference between level 35 and level 50 attributes is enormous.

### Priority 9: Weapons Workshop (Level 5+)

- **Why**: Weapon modifications and custom designs unlock at higher levels. The 50%+ discount also saves significant Credits when equipping a large roster.

### Priority 10: Booking Office

- **Why**: Unlocks event subscriptions for your robots with facility-level-driven cap curve. Heavily prestige-gated but has zero operating cost.

```callout-warning
Late game facilities like the Tuning Bay have operating costs of ₡3,000/day at Level 10. Even with Champion-tier income, running every facility at max level will be expensive. Prioritize the facilities that match your competitive goals.
```

## Facilities to Skip or Delay

Not every facility is worth investing in at every stage:

| Facility | When to Skip | When to Buy |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Storage Facility | Until you run out of weapon slots | When you have 4+ robots with multiple loadout options |
| Booking Office | Until you need more event subscriptions | When you want to subscribe robots to multiple battle events |

## General Investment Principles

1. **ROI first, power second** — Facilities that save or make money should come before facilities that boost combat stats
2. **Match your strategy** — Don't invest in the AI Academy if none of your robots use AI attributes
3. **Watch operating costs** — Every facility is an ongoing expense. Don't let total daily costs exceed 50% of your daily income
4. **Prestige gates matter** — Plan around prestige requirements. There's no point saving for a Level 7 academy if you're 3,000 Prestige short
5. **Compound investments** — The Training Facility + Academies combo is more valuable than either alone. The Streaming Studio + Fame-building is more valuable than either alone.

```callout-tip
Check your Financial Report regularly. If your total facility operating costs are climbing above 40% of your daily income, slow down on new facility purchases and focus on promoting robots to higher tiers to boost income first.
```

## Understanding the Investment Overview Tab

The **Investment Overview** tab on the Facilities page provides a consolidated view of your economic facility performance:

- **Lifetime metrics**: Total investment, total returns, operating costs, and net ROI for each of the 5 economic facilities
- **Paid-off status**: A clear badge showing whether each facility has earned back its investment
- **Projected payoff**: For facilities not yet paid off, an estimate of how many more cycles until breakeven
- **Data source indicator**: Shows whether calculations use actual cycle snapshot data ("Historical") or formula-based estimates ("Estimated")
- **Upgrade recommendations**: Prioritized suggestions for which facilities to upgrade next, based on your current game state

The ROI calculations use your actual cycle-by-cycle financial data (from `CycleSnapshot.stableMetrics`) when available. For facilities purchased before snapshot tracking began, formula-based estimates provide reasonable approximations.
