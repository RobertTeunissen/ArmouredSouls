## Overview

Your stable can house up to **15 different facilities**, each providing a unique benefit — from slashing repair bills to unlocking higher attribute caps. Every facility has up to 10 upgrade levels (except Roster Expansion, which caps at 9), and each level increases both the benefit and the daily operating cost.

Facilities are your long-term investments. Unlike one-time purchases like weapons or attribute upgrades, facilities generate ongoing value every single cycle — but they also charge ongoing operating costs. Choosing which facilities to invest in, and when, is one of the most important strategic decisions in the game.

![Facility overview grid](/images/guide/facilities/facility-overview-grid.png)

```callout-info
Facilities are grouped into four categories: Economy & Discounts, Capacity & Storage, Training Academies, and Advanced Features. This guide covers all 15 at a glance — see the individual articles for deep dives on level progression and ROI.
```

## Economy & Discount Facilities

These facilities reduce your operating costs or generate passive income. They tend to have the best early-game ROI.

| Facility | Purpose | Level 1 Cost | Operating Cost | Key Benefit |
|----------|---------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Training Facility | Attribute upgrade discounts | ₡150,000 | level × ₡250/day | 10%–90% discount on attribute upgrades |
| Weapons Workshop | Weapon purchase discounts | ₡75,000 | level × ₡100/day | 10%–100% discount on weapon purchases |
| Repair Bay | Repair cost discounts | ₡50,000 | level × ₡100/day | Scales with level and robot count (up to 90% discount) |
| Merchandising Hub | Passive merchandising income | ₡150,000 | level × ₡200/day | ₡5,000–₡50,000/day base income, scales with Prestige |
| Streaming Studio | Streaming revenue multiplier | ₡100,000 | level × ₡100/day | 2×–11× streaming revenue per battle |

### Training Facility

The Training Facility provides a percentage discount on all attribute upgrade costs. At Level 1 you get 10% off; at Level 9 (the maximum discount level) you get 90% off. Given that maxing all 23 attributes on a single robot costs over ₡29 million at full price, even a modest discount saves enormous amounts over time.

**Discount formula**: Level × 10%, capped at 90%

### Weapons Workshop

Reduces the purchase price of weapons from the shop. Higher levels also unlock weapon modifications (Level 3) and custom weapon design (Level 6). At Level 10, weapons are free.

**Discount formula**: Level × 10%

### Repair Bay

The Repair Bay is unique because its discount scales with both its level and the number of active robots in your stable. More robots means a bigger discount per Repair Bay level.

**Discount formula**: Level × (5 + Active Robot Count), capped at 90%

For example, with 4 robots and Repair Bay Level 3: 3 × (5 + 4) = 27% discount. With 10 robots and Level 6, you hit the 90% cap.

```callout-tip
The Repair Bay becomes dramatically more cost-effective as you expand your roster. If you're running 3+ robots, prioritize Repair Bay upgrades — the multi-robot discount formula rewards roster expansion.
```

### Merchandising Hub

Unlocks passive daily income from merchandising. The base income is ₡5,000 per level per day, but it scales with your stable's [Prestige](/guide/prestige-fame/prestige-system). At high Prestige levels, merchandising becomes a significant income stream that requires zero effort.

**Income formula**: (Level × ₡5,000) × (1 + Prestige / 10,000)

### Streaming Studio

Multiplies the streaming revenue your robots earn from every battle. Each level adds 100% to the multiplier — Level 1 doubles streaming revenue, Level 5 gives 6× revenue, and Level 10 gives 11× revenue. The operating cost is very low (₡100/day per level), making this one of the most efficient income-generating facilities.

**Multiplier formula**: 1 + (Level × 1.0)

## Capacity & Storage Facilities

These facilities expand what your stable can hold. They're essential for growth.

| Facility | Purpose | Level 1 Cost | Operating Cost | Key Benefit |
|----------|---------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Roster Expansion | More robot slots | ₡150,000 | ₡500/day per slot beyond first | 1→10 robot slots (9 levels max) |
| Storage Facility | More weapon storage | ₡75,000 | ₡500 + ₡250/level beyond first | 5→55 weapon slots |

### Roster Expansion

Every player starts with 1 robot slot. Each level of Roster Expansion adds one more slot, up to a maximum of 10 robots at Level 9. More robots means more battles, more income streams, and better Repair Bay discounts — but also more repair costs and operating expenses.

**Capacity formula**: 1 + Level (max 10 at Level 9)

### Storage Facility

Increases the total number of weapons your stable can own (both equipped and unequipped). You start with 5 weapon slots and gain 5 more per level, up to 55 at Level 10.

**Capacity formula**: 5 + (Level × 5)

## Training Academies

Four academies control the attribute caps for each attribute category. Without academies, all attributes are capped at level 10. See the [Training Academies Guide](/guide/facilities/training-academies) for the full breakdown.

| Facility | Attribute Category | Attributes Covered | Level 1 Cost | Operating Cost |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------|----------------|
| Combat Training Academy | Combat Systems | 6 attributes | ₡100,000 | level × ₡250/day |
| Defense Training Academy | Defensive Systems | 5 attributes | ₡100,000 | level × ₡250/day |
| Mobility Training Academy | Chassis & Mobility | 5 attributes | ₡100,000 | level × ₡250/day |
| AI Training Academy | AI Processing + Team Coordination | 7 attributes | ₡100,000 | level × ₡250/day |

All four academies follow the same pricing structure: ₡100,000 per level, with prestige gates at Levels 3, 5, 7, 9, and 10. Each level raises the attribute cap by 3–5 points, from a base cap of 10 up to a maximum of 50.

```callout-info
Training Academies are the only way to push attributes past level 10. If you want competitive robots in higher leagues, you'll need to invest in the academies that cover your build's key attributes.
```

## Advanced Facilities

These facilities unlock special features and late-game mechanics. Some are not yet fully implemented.

| Facility | Purpose | Level 1 Cost | Operating Cost | Key Benefit |
|----------|---------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Research Lab | Analytics & tools | ₡200,000 | ₡2,000 + ₡1,000/level | Not yet implemented |
| Medical Bay | Critical damage reduction | ₡175,000 | ₡2,000 + ₡1,000/level | Not yet implemented |
| Coaching Staff | Stable-wide stat bonuses | ₡250,000 | ₡3,000/day when active | Not yet implemented |
| Booking Office | Tournament access & rewards | ₡250,000 | None | Not yet implemented |

### Research Lab

Unlocks analytical tools: battle analytics (Level 1), loadout presets (Level 2), AI behavior customization (Level 3), battle simulation (Level 5), and more. Expensive to operate but invaluable for optimizing your strategy at higher tiers. This facility is not yet implemented.

### Medical Bay

Reduces repair costs specifically for **critical damage** — when a robot's HP hits zero. This is separate from the Repair Bay, which handles regular battle damage. At Level 10, critical damage penalties are eliminated entirely. This facility is not yet implemented.

### Coaching Staff

Lets you hire coaches that provide stable-wide attribute bonuses. See the [Coaching Staff Guide](/guide/facilities/coaching-staff) for the planned system breakdown. This facility is not yet implemented.

### Booking Office

Planned to unlock access to tiered tournaments and enhanced tournament rewards. Currently all tournaments are open to every eligible robot regardless of Prestige level. This facility is not yet implemented and has no operating cost.
