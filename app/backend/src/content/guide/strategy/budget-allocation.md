## Overview

Budget allocation is the art of deciding where your credits go. Every cycle, you earn income and face expenses — and the choices you make about how to invest the surplus determine whether your stable grows or stagnates.

There's no single correct allocation. The right split depends on your game stage, roster size, league tiers, and strategic goals. But there are proven frameworks that help you avoid the most common mistakes.

## The Four Budget Categories

Every credit you spend falls into one of four categories:

| Category | What It Covers | Priority |
|----------|---------------|----------|
| **Reserves** | Emergency fund for repairs and operating costs | Always first |
| **Attributes** | Robot stat upgrades (Combat Power, Armor, etc.) | Core investment |
| **Facilities** | Buildings that generate passive income or bonuses | Long-term growth |
| **Expansion** | New robots, weapons, roster growth | When stable |

```callout-warning
Never spend your last credit. Always maintain a reserve of at least 3–5 cycles of operating expenses. Running out of credits means bankruptcy, and recovery is painful.
```

## Early Game Allocation (Cycles 1–30)

You start with ₡3,000,000. The early game is about establishing a foundation — getting your first robots competitive and building a sustainable income stream.

| Category | Recommended % | Reasoning |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Reserves | 20% | Buffer against bad luck and repair spikes |
| Attributes | 50% | Your robots need stats to win battles and earn income |
| Facilities | 20% | One or two key facilities for income generation |
| Expansion | 10% | Maybe a second robot once your first is stable |

Focus attribute spending on your primary archetype stats. A robot that's great at one thing beats a robot that's mediocre at everything. See [Build Archetypes](/guide/strategy/build-archetypes) for recommended attribute priorities.

```callout-tip
In the early game, battle winnings are your primary income. Every attribute point that helps you win more fights pays for itself quickly. Prioritize combat effectiveness over passive income facilities.
```

## Mid Game Allocation (Cycles 30–100)

By mid game, you should have 2–3 robots in Silver or Gold tier, at least one income-generating facility, and a growing Prestige level. The focus shifts toward diversification and facility investment.

| Category | Recommended % | Reasoning |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Reserves | 15% | Lower percentage but higher absolute amount |
| Attributes | 35% | Still important, but diminishing returns at higher levels |
| Facilities | 35% | Facilities compound — invest now for long-term payoff |
| Expansion | 15% | Third and fourth robots to diversify income |

Facility investment becomes critical here. A well-timed Streaming Studio or Merchandising Hub can dramatically increase your passive income, reducing dependence on battle results.

## Late Game Allocation (Cycles 100+)

Late game is about optimization. Your robots are in Platinum+ tiers, facilities are generating significant passive income, and you're fine-tuning rather than building from scratch.

| Category | Recommended % | Reasoning |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Reserves | 10% | Stable income means less emergency buffer needed |
| Attributes | 25% | Targeted upgrades for specific matchup advantages |
| Facilities | 40% | Max-level facilities provide the best ROI |
| Expansion | 25% | Specialist robots for tournament play |

```callout-info
At high attribute levels, each upgrade costs more but provides the same flat bonus. This is where facility investment overtakes attribute spending in terms of ROI — passive income scales without diminishing returns.
```

## Common Mistakes

- **Spending everything on attributes** — Leaves no buffer for bad cycles and no facility income
- **Building too many facilities early** — Operating costs eat into your thin early-game margins
- **Expanding too fast** — More robots means more repair costs and more attribute investment needed
- **Ignoring reserves** — One bad cycle can cascade into bankruptcy without a safety net
