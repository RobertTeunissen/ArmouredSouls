## Overview

Streaming revenue comes from broadcasting your robots' battles to fans and viewers. Unlike [merchandising](/guide/economy/merchandising) (which is stable-level and driven by Prestige), streaming income is generated **per-robot** based on each robot's individual **Fame**, their **battle activity**, and whether your stable has a **Streaming Studio** facility.

The more your robots fight and the more famous they become, the larger their streaming audience grows — and the more revenue each broadcast generates.

## How Streaming Revenue Works

Streaming income is calculated once per cycle during the daily processing. Three factors determine how much each robot earns:

### 1. Number of Battles

More battles means more content to stream. A robot that fights every cycle generates more streaming revenue than one that sits idle. This is the most direct lever — if your robot is enlisted in league battles, it's producing streamable content.

| Battle Activity | Streaming Impact |
|----------------|-----------------|
| **No battles** | No streaming revenue for that cycle — nothing to broadcast |
| **1 battle per cycle** | Baseline streaming income |
| **Multiple battles** (tournaments + league) | Higher streaming income — more content means more viewers |

```callout-tip
Streaming revenue rewards active play. Make sure all your robots are enlisted in league battles every cycle. Tournament participation provides additional streaming content on top of regular league fights.
```

### 2. Fame Level

A robot's **Fame** determines how many viewers tune in to watch their battles. Higher Fame means a larger audience, which directly translates to more streaming revenue.

| Fame Tier | Streaming Impact |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Unknown** | Minimal viewers — streaming income is negligible |
| **Known** | Small but growing audience — streaming starts to generate noticeable income |
| **Famous** | Solid viewership — streaming becomes a meaningful income source |
| **Renowned** | Large audience — streaming revenue is a significant contributor to your finances |
| **Legendary** | Massive viewership — streaming income rivals battle winnings for this robot |
| **Mythical** | Peak audience — maximum streaming revenue, this robot is a celebrity |

Fame grows through battle performance. Winning battles, achieving dominant victories, and pulling off comebacks all build Fame faster than ordinary performances. See the [Fame Tiers Guide](/guide/prestige-fame/fame-tiers) for details on how Fame accumulates.

```callout-info
Fame is per-robot, not per-stable. Each robot builds its own reputation independently. A stable with three Famous robots generates more total streaming revenue than a stable with one Legendary robot and two Unknown ones — because all three are contributing.
```

### 3. Streaming Studio Facility

The **Streaming Studio** facility is required to unlock streaming revenue and amplifies it at higher levels. Without a Streaming Studio, your robots don't generate any streaming income regardless of their Fame or battle activity.

- **No Streaming Studio** — Zero streaming revenue
- **Level 1** — Enables streaming income at a baseline rate
- **Higher levels** — Each upgrade increases the streaming revenue multiplier, making your robots' Fame and battle activity more profitable

The Streaming Studio has a daily operating cost, so it needs to generate enough streaming revenue to justify its upkeep. This depends on having robots with decent Fame and consistent battle activity.

```callout-warning
Don't build a Streaming Studio too early. If your robots are all Unknown Fame in Bronze league, the streaming income won't cover the facility's operating costs. Wait until at least one robot has reached Known or Famous tier before investing.
```

## Building Streaming Revenue Over Time

Streaming income grows naturally as your robots progress:

1. **Early game** — Robots start with Unknown Fame and fight in Bronze. Streaming income is negligible even with a Studio. Focus on battle winnings instead.
2. **Mid game** — As robots climb to Silver/Gold and build Fame through victories, streaming income becomes noticeable. This is when the Streaming Studio starts paying for itself.
3. **Late game** — Robots with high Fame in upper tiers generate substantial streaming revenue. Combined with a high-level Streaming Studio, this becomes a major income source.

```callout-tip
Fame builds faster with impressive performances. Perfect victories (winning without taking damage) grant 2× Fame, dominating victories grant 1.5×, and comeback victories grant 1.25×. Invest in builds that can achieve these outcomes to accelerate your streaming income growth.
```

## Streaming vs Merchandising

Both are passive income streams, but they work differently:

| Aspect | Streaming | Merchandising |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Driven by** | Robot Fame + battle activity | Stable Prestige |
| **Scope** | Per-robot | Per-stable |
| **Requires facility** | Yes (Streaming Studio) | Optional (Merchandising Hub amplifies) |
| **Activity dependent** | Yes — no battles, no income | No — passive regardless of activity |
| **Best for** | Multi-robot stables with active fighters | Established stables with high Prestige |

A well-rounded stable invests in both. Merchandising provides a consistent baseline, while streaming rewards active play and scales with your roster size.

## Maximizing Streaming Income

- **Keep all robots enlisted** — Every battle generates streaming content
- **Build Fame through performance** — Dominant victories and comebacks build Fame faster
- **Upgrade the Streaming Studio** — Higher levels multiply all streaming revenue
- **Run multiple robots** — Each robot generates its own streaming income, so more robots means more total revenue
- **Compete in tournaments** — Tournament battles provide additional streaming content beyond regular league fights
