## Overview

Income is only half the financial picture. Your stable has ongoing expenses that are deducted every cycle. Understanding and managing these costs is essential to staying profitable — especially in the early game when income is limited.

There are two main categories of expenses: **facility operating costs** (fixed daily fees) and **repair costs** (variable, based on battle damage).

## Facility Operating Costs

Every facility in your stable charges a **daily operating cost** to keep running. This fee is deducted every cycle regardless of whether you actively use the facility that day.

For the complete facility-by-facility breakdown of operating costs at every level, see the [Facility Progression & ROI Guide](/guide/facilities/facility-progression). Here's a quick overview of what each facility costs to run:

| Facility | Level 1 Cost | Level 10 Cost | Status |
|----------|-------------|---------------|--------|
| Repair Bay | ₡1,000/day | ₡5,500/day | Implemented |
| Training Facility | ₡250/day | N/A (max L9) | Implemented |
| Weapons Workshop | ₡1,000/day | ₡5,500/day | Implemented |
| Merchandising Hub | ₡200/day | ₡2,000/day | Implemented |

```callout-warning
Facility costs add up fast. If you own 5 facilities at mid-level, you could be paying ₡5,000–₡15,000 per cycle just in operating costs. Only invest in facilities that provide clear value for your current strategy.
```

### Managing Facility Costs

- **Don't overbuild early** — Each facility is an ongoing expense. In the early game, stick to 2–3 essential facilities (Training Facility, Repair Bay) and expand as your income grows.
- **Higher levels cost more** — Upgrading a facility increases its daily operating cost. Make sure the benefit justifies the increased expense.
- **Some facilities pay for themselves** — The Merchandising Hub and Streaming Studio generate income that can offset their operating costs, but only once your Prestige and Fame are high enough.

## Repair Costs

After every battle, damaged robots need repairs. Repair costs are **variable** — they depend on how much damage your robot took and several other factors.

### What Influences Repair Costs

Several factors combine to determine your repair bill after each battle:

#### Robot Attributes

Repair costs are based on the **sum of all 23 attributes** of the robot being repaired, combined with how much damage was taken. A robot with higher attributes costs more to repair per point of damage. As you upgrade your robots and push into higher leagues, your repair bills naturally increase — not because of the league tier itself, but because your robots have more invested in them.

On top of that, there's a destruction multiplier. If your robot gets completely destroyed (HP hits 0), the repair bill is significantly higher than if it survived with even a sliver of health. Robots that are heavily damaged but still standing also pay a smaller penalty. Surrendering before destruction via your [Yield Threshold](/guide/combat/yielding-and-repair-costs) is the main way to control this.

```callout-tip
Your Yield Threshold setting directly controls how much damage your robot takes before surrendering. A higher yield threshold means your robot surrenders earlier, taking less damage and costing less to repair — but also potentially missing out on comeback victories.
```

Defensive attributes also play a role:

- **Armor Plating & Shield Capacity** — Better defenses mean less hull damage taken per battle, which directly reduces repair costs.
- **Self-Repair Systems** — This attribute provides a direct reduction to repair costs. Higher levels mean cheaper repairs across the board.

```callout-info
Investing in defensive attributes doesn't just help you win battles — it also saves you money on repairs. A well-armored robot that wins with 80% HP remaining costs far less to repair than a glass cannon that wins with 10% HP.
```

### Manual Repair Discount

When you manually repair your robots using the **Repair All** button on the Robots page, you receive a **50% discount** on the total repair cost. This discount is applied on top of any Repair Bay savings, so the two stack together for significant savings.

Automatic repairs that happen during the daily cycle do **not** receive this discount — they're charged at the standard rate. This means actively managing your repairs between cycles is one of the most effective ways to save Credits.

```callout-tip
Always repair your robots manually before the next cycle starts. The 50% manual repair discount can save you thousands of Credits per cycle, especially if you're running multiple robots with heavy battle damage.
```

### The Repair Bay Facility

The **Repair Bay** facility reduces repair costs for all your robots. At higher levels, it provides increasingly significant discounts. If you're running multiple robots, the Repair Bay also offers a multi-robot discount that makes it one of the most cost-effective early investments.

Combined with the manual repair discount, a well-upgraded Repair Bay can reduce your repair bills dramatically — making aggressive play much more affordable.

See the [Repair Bay Guide](/guide/facilities/repair-bay) for details on level progression and cost savings.

## Total Daily Expenses

Your total daily expenses are the sum of all your facility operating costs plus all your robot repair costs. This total is deducted from your balance each cycle, after all income has been credited. If your expenses exceed your income for a cycle, your balance decreases.

```callout-warning
Track your expenses-to-income ratio. If your total daily expenses are consistently above 70–80% of your daily income, you have very little margin for error. One bad cycle of heavy battle damage could push you into the red.
```

## Cost Management Strategies

### Early Game

- Keep facilities minimal — Training Facility and Repair Bay are usually enough
- Set yield thresholds conservatively (higher %) to limit repair costs
- Focus on one or two robots to keep total repair bills manageable
- **Repair manually** between cycles to take advantage of the 50% manual repair discount

### Mid Game

- Add income-generating facilities (Merchandising Hub, Streaming Studio) only when your Prestige and Fame justify the operating cost
- Invest in defensive attributes to reduce per-battle repair costs
- Consider the Self-Repair Systems attribute for robots that frequently take heavy damage
- Combine the Repair Bay discount with manual repairs for maximum savings

### Late Game

- Higher-tier rewards should comfortably cover all operating costs
- Optimize yield thresholds based on your financial cushion — you can afford to fight more aggressively
- Facility operating costs become a smaller percentage of total income as battle rewards scale up
- Even at high income levels, manual repairs still save meaningful Credits over automatic repairs
