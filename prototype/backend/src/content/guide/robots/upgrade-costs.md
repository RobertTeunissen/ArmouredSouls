## Overview

Upgrading your robot's attributes is the primary way to make it stronger — but it's not free. Each attribute level costs Credits, and the cost increases as the attribute gets higher. Understanding the cost curve and how to reduce it is key to managing your stable's finances effectively.

## How Upgrade Costs Scale

The formula for upgrading an attribute from level N to level N+1 is:

```
cost = (current_level + 1) × ₡1,500
```

So upgrading from level 1→2 costs ₡3,000, from level 9→10 costs ₡15,000, and from level 49→50 costs ₡75,000. The cost grows linearly — no exponential surprises, but it adds up fast when you're upgrading many attributes across multiple robots.

### Per-Level Cost Table

| From → To | Cost | Cumulative from Level 1 |
|-----------|------|------------------------|
| 1 → 2 | ₡3,000 | ₡3,000 |
| 2 → 3 | ₡4,500 | ₡7,500 |
| 3 → 4 | ₡6,000 | ₡13,500 |
| 4 → 5 | ₡7,500 | ₡21,000 |
| 5 → 6 | ₡9,000 | ₡30,000 |
| 6 → 7 | ₡10,500 | ₡40,500 |
| 7 → 8 | ₡12,000 | ₡52,500 |
| 8 → 9 | ₡13,500 | ₡66,000 |
| 9 → 10 | ₡15,000 | ₡81,000 |
| 10 → 11 | ₡16,500 | ₡97,500 |
| 19 → 20 | ₡30,000 | ₡297,000 |
| 29 → 30 | ₡45,000 | ₡672,000 |
| 39 → 40 | ₡60,000 | ₡1,197,000 |
| 49 → 50 | ₡75,000 | ₡1,911,000 |

```callout-info
Getting one attribute from 1 to 10 costs ₡81,000. Getting it from 1 to 50 costs ₡1,911,000 — about 24× more. The cost scaling is linear per level, but the cumulative total grows quadratically. This is why the Training Facility discount matters so much at higher levels.
```

## Training Facility Discount

The **Training Facility** is one of the most impactful facilities you can invest in. Each level provides a **10% discount** on all attribute upgrade costs, stacking up to a maximum of 90% off at the highest levels.

| Training Facility Level | Discount | Effective Cost |
|------------------------|----------|---------------|
| Level 0 (none) | 0% | Full price |
| Level 1 | 10% | 90% of base cost |
| Level 2 | 20% | 80% of base cost |
| Level 3 | 30% | 70% of base cost |
| Level 4 | 40% | 60% of base cost |
| Level 5 | 50% | Half price |
| Level 6 | 60% | 40% of base cost |
| Level 7 | 70% | 30% of base cost |
| Level 8 | 80% | 20% of base cost |
| Level 9 | 90% | 10% of base cost |

```callout-tip
The Training Facility is one of the best early investments in the game. Even Level 1 (costing ₡150,000) immediately saves you 10% on every attribute upgrade across all your robots. If you're actively upgrading — and you should be — this pays for itself quickly.
```

### When to Invest in Training Facility

The Training Facility has a daily operating cost that scales with its level (₡250 per day per level). Here's how to think about the investment:

- **Level 1 (₡150,000, ₡250/day)**: Buy this early. The 10% discount pays for itself as soon as you're spending ₡2,500 or more per day on upgrades — which happens almost immediately
- **Levels 2–3**: Upgrade as your income grows. The 20–30% discount makes mid-range attribute upgrades much more affordable
- **Levels 4–5**: Mid-game priority. At 40–50% discount, expensive upgrades become half price. Level 4 requires 1,000 Prestige
- **Levels 6–9**: Late-game optimization. The discount is enormous, but operating costs are higher and some levels require significant Prestige thresholds

```callout-warning
Higher Training Facility levels require Prestige thresholds to unlock (Level 4 needs 1,000 Prestige, Level 7 needs 5,000, Level 9 needs 10,000). Plan your Prestige progression alongside your facility investments.
```

### Discount Impact on Total Build Cost

The Training Facility discount applies to every attribute upgrade for every robot in your stable. The savings compound dramatically:

- **1 robot, levels 1→10 on 14 active attributes**: Without discount: 14 × ₡81,000 = ₡1,134,000. With Level 1 Training Facility (10% off): 14 × ₡72,900 = ₡1,020,600. Savings: ₡113,400.
- **3 robots, all upgrading actively**: The savings triple, making the facility investment pay back even faster
- **Late game, pushing attributes to 30+**: Without the discount, high-level upgrades are painfully expensive. With a Level 7+ Training Facility, they become manageable

## Upgrade Strategy Tips

### Early Game (Starting Budget)

With your initial ₡3,000,000, prioritize:

1. **Buy Training Facility Level 1** (₡150,000) — immediate 10% savings on everything
2. **Focus upgrades on the 14 active attributes** rather than all 23 (see [Attributes Overview](/guide/robots/attributes-overview) for which are active)
3. **Get key attributes to level 10** — at ₡81,000 per attribute (₡72,900 with Training Facility), this is the initial cap before academies and gives strong returns
4. **Prioritize 6–8 core attributes** for your build rather than spreading thin across all 14

With the 1-robot strategy (₡1,550,000 for attributes), you can comfortably get all 14 active attributes to level 10 with the Training Facility discount (14 × ₡72,900 = ₡1,020,600). With 3 robots (₡650,000 total), you'll need to be more selective — focus on 6–8 key attributes per robot.

### Mid Game

- Upgrade Training Facility to Level 3–4 for 30–40% discount
- Start pushing key attributes into the 15–25 range
- Invest in [Training Academies](/guide/robots/training-academies) to unlock attribute caps beyond level 10
- Consider which attributes give the best return for your build archetype

### Late Game

- Push Training Facility toward Level 7+ for massive discounts
- Focus on maxing out your most impactful attributes (the ones that define your build)
- The cost of pushing attributes from 40 to 50 is high even with discounts — be selective about which attributes you max

```callout-info
You don't need to max every attribute. Most competitive builds focus on 8–12 key attributes at high levels, with the rest at moderate levels. The cost of going from level 40 to 50 on a single attribute (₡714,000 at full price) could fund multiple attributes from level 1 to 10 (₡81,000 each).
```
