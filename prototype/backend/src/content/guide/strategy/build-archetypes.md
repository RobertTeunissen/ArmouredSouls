## Overview

Every robot in Armoured Souls can be built around a core strategy. While there's room for hybrid approaches, most competitive builds fall into one of five proven archetypes. Each archetype emphasizes different attributes, weapons, loadouts, and stances to create a distinct combat identity.

No archetype is strictly "best" — each has strengths and weaknesses, and the meta shifts as you climb through league tiers. The right choice depends on your playstyle, budget, and what you're facing in your league instance.

## The Five Archetypes

### 1. Tank

The Tank prioritizes survival. High armor, strong shields, and massive hull HP let this build absorb punishment and outlast opponents. Tanks win through attrition — they may not hit the hardest, but they're still standing when the other robot isn't.

![Tank archetype](/images/guide/strategy/tank-archetype.png)

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Primary Attributes** | Hull Integrity, Armor Plating, Energy Shield Capacity, Damage Dampeners |
| **Secondary Attributes** | Counter Protocols, Combat Power |
| **Recommended Loadout** | Weapon + Shield |
| **Recommended Stance** | Defensive |
| **Recommended Weapons** | Energy or Ballistic main weapon + Shield offhand |
| **Primary Academy** | Defense Training Academy |

**Strengths**:
- Extremely durable — survives long fights
- Low repair costs (takes less hull damage)
- Consistent performance — rarely gets destroyed
- Strong against Glass Cannons (absorbs burst damage)

**Weaknesses**:
- Low damage output — fights take longer
- Vulnerable to Snipers (high penetration bypasses armor)
- Slower Fame growth (fewer dominating victories)

```callout-tip
Tanks excel in the early game when opponents lack the penetration to break through your defenses. As you climb tiers, invest in Counter Protocols to punish attackers while you absorb their hits.
```

### 2. Glass Cannon

Maximum damage, minimum defense. The Glass Cannon hits incredibly hard but can't take a punch. This build wins by destroying opponents before they can deal meaningful damage back.

![Glass Cannon archetype](/images/guide/strategy/glass-cannon-archetype.png)

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Primary Attributes** | Combat Power, Targeting Systems, Critical Systems, Penetration |
| **Secondary Attributes** | Attack Speed, Weapon Control |
| **Recommended Loadout** | Two-Handed |
| **Recommended Stance** | Offensive |
| **Recommended Weapons** | High-damage two-handed weapon (Energy or Ballistic) |
| **Primary Academy** | Combat Training Academy |

**Strengths**:
- Highest damage output of any archetype
- Fast kills — ends fights quickly
- High Fame growth (frequent dominating/perfect victories)
- Strong against Tanks (overwhelms defenses)

**Weaknesses**:
- Fragile — a few hits can be devastating
- High repair costs when things go wrong
- Vulnerable to Speed Demons (can't hit what they can't catch)
- Inconsistent — great when winning, expensive when losing

```callout-warning
Glass Cannons live and die by their damage output. If your opponent survives the initial burst, you're in trouble. Set a moderate [yield threshold](/guide/strategy/yield-strategy) to avoid catastrophic repair bills from fights that go sideways.
```

### 3. Speed Demon

Speed Demons rely on evasion and attack speed to dodge incoming attacks while landing rapid strikes. They don't hit as hard per attack, but they attack more often and are harder to hit.

![Speed Demon archetype](/images/guide/strategy/speed-demon-archetype.png)

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Primary Attributes** | Servo Motors, Evasion Thrusters, Attack Speed, Gyro Stabilizers |
| **Secondary Attributes** | Combat Power, Targeting Systems |
| **Recommended Loadout** | Dual-Wield |
| **Recommended Stance** | Balanced |
| **Recommended Weapons** | Fast Melee or Energy weapons in both hands |
| **Primary Academy** | Mobility Training Academy |

**Strengths**:
- Hard to hit — high evasion reduces incoming damage
- Multiple attacks per round (dual-wield + attack speed)
- Good Fame growth (evasion leads to high-HP victories)
- Strong against Glass Cannons (dodges their big hits)

**Weaknesses**:
- Lower per-hit damage
- Offhand attacks have reduced accuracy (50% base hit chance)
- Vulnerable to Tanks (can't punch through heavy armor)
- Requires investment in multiple attribute categories

```callout-tip
Speed Demons benefit enormously from the Dual-Wield loadout's per-hand bonuses. Make sure both weapons complement your attribute spread — fast weapons with low cooldowns maximize your attack frequency advantage.
```

### 4. Counter Striker

The Counter Striker is a reactive build that punishes opponents for attacking. High Counter Protocols and defensive stats let this build absorb hits and retaliate automatically, turning the opponent's aggression against them.

![Counter Striker archetype](/images/guide/strategy/counter-striker-archetype.png)

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Primary Attributes** | Counter Protocols, Armor Plating, Energy Shield Capacity, Combat Power |
| **Secondary Attributes** | Damage Dampeners, Hull Integrity |
| **Recommended Loadout** | Weapon + Shield |
| **Recommended Stance** | Defensive |
| **Recommended Weapons** | Melee main weapon + Shield offhand |
| **Primary Academy** | Defense Training Academy |

**Strengths**:
- Punishes aggressive opponents automatically
- Strong against Speed Demons (counter-attacks trigger on each attack, hit or miss)
- Good durability from defensive stats
- Efficient damage — counter-attacks don't use your attack turn

**Weaknesses**:
- Passive — relies on opponent attacking first
- Weak against Snipers (few attacks to counter)
- Counter-attack damage scales with your own combat stats (needs investment in both defense and offense)

### 5. Sniper

The Sniper focuses on precision — high targeting, critical hit chance, and penetration to land devastating single shots that bypass defenses. Snipers don't need to hit often; they just need every hit to count.

![Sniper archetype](/images/guide/strategy/sniper-archetype.png)

| Aspect | Recommendation |
|--------|---------------|
| **Primary Attributes** | Targeting Systems, Critical Systems, Penetration, Combat Power |
| **Secondary Attributes** | Combat Algorithms, Threat Analysis |
| **Recommended Loadout** | Single |
| **Recommended Stance** | Offensive |
| **Recommended Weapons** | High-damage single weapon (Ballistic preferred) |
| **Primary Academy** | Combat Training Academy |

**Strengths**:
- Highest per-hit damage (critical hits + penetration)
- Bypasses armor effectively
- Strong against Tanks (penetration negates armor advantage)
- Single loadout bonus amplifies weapon effectiveness

**Weaknesses**:
- Fewer attacks per round (single weapon, no dual-wield)
- Vulnerable to Speed Demons (hard to hit evasive targets)
- Inconsistent — relies on critical hits landing
- No shield for defense

## Archetype Matchup Summary

| Attacker ↓ / Defender → | Tank | Glass Cannon | Speed Demon | Counter Striker | Sniper |
|--------------------------|------|-------------|-------------|----------------|--------|
| **Tank** | Draw | Favorable | Unfavorable | Even | Unfavorable |
| **Glass Cannon** | Unfavorable | Even | Unfavorable | Even | Favorable |
| **Speed Demon** | Favorable | Favorable | Even | Unfavorable | Favorable |
| **Counter Striker** | Even | Even | Favorable | Draw | Unfavorable |
| **Sniper** | Favorable | Unfavorable | Unfavorable | Favorable | Even |

```callout-info
No archetype dominates all matchups. The best strategy is to understand what you're likely to face in your league instance and build accordingly — or run multiple robots with different archetypes to cover your weaknesses.
```
