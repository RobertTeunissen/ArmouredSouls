## Overview

Your robot's [yield threshold](/guide/combat/yielding-and-repair-costs) determines when it surrenders during battle. This single setting has cascading effects on your repair costs, win rate, Fame growth, and tournament eligibility. Getting it right for your build and financial situation is one of the most impactful strategic decisions you can make.

## The Core Trade-Off

| Setting | Effect | Pros | Cons |
|---------|--------|------|------|
| **Low yield** (e.g., 5–15%) | Robot fights until nearly destroyed | More comeback victories, higher win rate in close fights | Expensive repairs, risk of destruction |
| **High yield** (e.g., 35–50%) | Robot surrenders early | Low repair costs, preserves HP | Surrenders winnable fights, fewer victories, slower Fame growth |
| **Moderate yield** (e.g., 20–30%) | Balanced approach | Reasonable repair costs, decent win rate | Doesn't excel at either extreme |

```callout-info
There's no universally "correct" yield threshold. The right setting depends on your build archetype, financial situation, league tier, and whether you're prioritizing tournaments.
```

## Yield Strategy by Archetype

### Tank (Recommended: 15–25%)

Tanks are built to absorb damage. Their high armor and shields mean they take less hull damage per hit, so fighting at low HP is less expensive for them than for other builds. A low yield threshold lets Tanks leverage their durability advantage fully.

- Repair costs stay manageable thanks to defensive stats
- Tanks rarely get destroyed even at low HP
- Low yield maximizes the attrition advantage

### Glass Cannon (Recommended: 35–50%)

Glass Cannons can't afford to take heavy damage. If the initial burst doesn't win the fight, staying in longer just racks up repair bills. A high yield threshold cuts losses on bad matchups.

- High repair costs when damaged (low defensive stats)
- Fights are usually decided quickly anyway
- Preserving HP matters more than marginal wins

```callout-warning
A Glass Cannon at 5% yield that loses a fight can cost more to repair than several battles' worth of winnings. Don't let pride override financial sense — surrender early when the fight isn't going your way.
```

### Speed Demon (Recommended: 25–40%)

Speed Demons take less damage overall thanks to evasion, but when they do get hit, it hurts. A moderate yield threshold balances their evasion advantage with the reality that a few solid hits can be devastating.

### Counter Striker (Recommended: 20–30%)

Counter Strikers need to take hits to trigger counter-attacks. A yield that's too high means fewer counter-attack opportunities. Keep it low enough to let the counter mechanic work, but not so low that repair costs spiral.

### Sniper (Recommended: 30–45%)

Snipers are fragile and rely on landing critical hits. If the fight drags on without crits connecting, it's better to surrender than absorb damage with no defensive stats to mitigate it.

## Financial Impact Analysis

The yield threshold directly affects your [repair costs](/guide/economy/repair-costs). Here's a simplified comparison for a Gold-tier robot:

| Yield Threshold | Average HP After Loss | Estimated Repair Cost | Battles Before Bankruptcy Risk |
|----------------|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| 5% | ~3% HP | ₡15,000–₡25,000 | Low margin |
| 15% | ~12% HP | ₡10,000–₡18,000 | Moderate margin |
| 30% | ~25% HP | ₡5,000–₡10,000 | Comfortable margin |
| 50% | ~45% HP | ₡2,000–₡5,000 | Very safe |

```callout-tip
In the early game when your budget is tight, err on the side of higher yield thresholds (35–50%). As your income grows and you can absorb repair costs comfortably, you can lower the threshold to win more fights.
```

## Adjusting Over Time

Your optimal yield threshold changes as your stable evolves:

| Game Stage | Recommended Approach | Why |
|-----------|---------------------|-----|
| Early game | Conservative (35–50%) | Budget is tight, can't afford big repair bills |
| Mid game | Moderate (20–35%) | Income supports moderate repairs, need wins for promotion |
| Late game | Aggressive (5–20%) | Income easily covers repairs, every win matters for LP |

```callout-tip
Review your yield threshold every time you promote to a new league tier. Higher tiers mean tougher opponents and more damage taken — you may need to adjust upward temporarily until your robot is competitive at the new level.
```
