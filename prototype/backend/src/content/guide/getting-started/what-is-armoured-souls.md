## You Are a Stable Manager

In Armoured Souls, you run a **stable** — a workshop, training ground, and business all in one. Your stable houses combat robots that fight in scheduled league battles, and your job is to make strategic decisions that turn a scrappy operation into a dominant force.

Think of it like being a football manager, except your players are robots and the pitch is an arena. You never control a robot directly in battle. Instead, you decide how they're built, how they're equipped, and how they fight — then the simulation engine handles the rest.

## Your Goal

There's no single way to "win" Armoured Souls. The game supports many different goals and playstyles:

- **Climb the ranks** — Push your robots from Bronze league all the way to Champion
- **Build a profitable stable** — Invest in facilities, grow your income streams, and run a financially sustainable operation
- **Earn prestige and fame** — Build your stable's reputation through victories, tournaments, and milestones
- **Master the meta** — Experiment with attribute builds, weapon loadouts, and stances to find optimal strategies
- **Compete in tournaments** — Enter single-elimination events for bonus rewards and bragging rights

Most players pursue a mix of these. The beauty is that they all feed into each other — winning battles earns credits, credits fund upgrades, upgrades lead to more wins, and wins earn prestige that unlocks new facilities.

## What You Actually Do

On a typical day, you'll spend 15–30 minutes:

1. **Reviewing results** from the last cycle — battle logs, financial reports, league standings
2. **Adjusting your robots** — upgrading attributes, swapping weapons, tweaking stances or yield thresholds
3. **Managing your stable** — upgrading facilities, buying new weapons, expanding your roster
4. **Planning ahead** — deciding where to invest your credits for the best return

Then the next cycle runs, your robots fight, and you come back to see how it went.

## Key Concepts

**Robots** are your fighters. Each one has 23 attributes across five categories (Combat, Defense, Mobility, AI, and Team Coordination), a weapon loadout, a battle stance, and a yield threshold. How you configure them determines how they perform.

**Facilities** are stable-wide upgrades that reduce costs, increase income, or unlock new capabilities. There are 15 facility types, each with 10 levels. Choosing which to invest in — and when — is a core strategic decision.

**Leagues** are the competitive structure. Robots are placed in one of six tiers (Bronze through Champion) and fight opponents within their tier. Perform well and your robot gets promoted. Struggle and they get demoted.

**Credits (₡)** are your primary resource. You earn them from battles, streaming revenue, and merchandising. You spend them on robot upgrades, weapons, facility investments, and repairs.

**Prestige** is your stable's reputation. It's earned through victories and milestones, never spent — but it unlocks higher facility levels and boosts your income.
