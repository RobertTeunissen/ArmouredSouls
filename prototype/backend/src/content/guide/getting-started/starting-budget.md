## Overview

Every new player starts with **₡3,000,000** (three million Credits). This is your seed money — it needs to cover your first robots, their weapons, initial attribute upgrades, and early facility investments. How you spend it shapes your early game experience.

The onboarding tutorial walks you through this step by step, but this page serves as a reference for the key costs and recommended allocations.

## What Things Cost

Here's a quick breakdown of the major expenses you'll face:

| Item | Cost | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Robot frame | ₡500,000 | Bare chassis with all 23 attributes at level 1 |
| Weapons | ₡50,000–₡544,000 | Practice Sword (₡50K) to Ion Beam (₡544K). Starter weapons at ₡50K for each range band. |
| Attribute upgrade (per level) | (level + 1) × ₡1,500 | Level 1→2 costs ₡3,000. Level 9→10 costs ₡15,000. Discounted by Training Facility. |
| Repair Bay (Level 1) | ₡50,000 | Reduces repair costs. Scales with number of robots. |
| Training Facility (Level 1) | ₡150,000 | 10% discount on all attribute upgrades. |
| Weapons Workshop (Level 1) | ₡75,000 | 10% discount on weapon purchases. |
| Roster Expansion (Level 1) | ₡150,000 | Required to own a 2nd robot. Level 2 (₡300,000) for a 3rd. |
| Training Academies (Level 1) | ₡100,000 each | Raises attribute caps from 10 to 15. Four types available. |

```callout-info
Robot frames are your biggest single expense at ₡500,000 each, and you will need to invest in Roster Expansion facilities in order to house them. The number of robots you buy determines how much budget remains for everything else.
```

## Spending Order Matters

The onboarding tutorial emphasizes a critical principle: **you can spend your money only once**. Discount facilities (Weapons Workshop, Training Facility) reduce the cost of future purchases. If you buy weapons before purchasing the Weapons Workshop, you pay full price and miss out on savings.

Always buy discount facilities first, then spend on the items they discount.

## Budget Allocations by Strategy

The onboarding flow recommends three roster strategies, each with a specific budget breakdown. These are guidelines, not strict rules — adjust based on your preferences.

### 1 Mighty Robot

Focus everything on a single powerful robot.

| Category | Amount | Details |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Facilities | ₡300,000 | Weapons Workshop, Training Facility, Repair Bay, plus optional academies |
| Robots | ₡500,000 | 1 robot frame |
| Weapons | ₡550,000 | 1–2 quality weapons for your chosen loadout |
| Attributes | ₡1,550,000 | Heavy investment in key attributes |
| Reserve | ₡50,000 | Covers immediate repairs and operating costs |

With only one robot, you don't need Roster Expansion and can pour credits into making that robot as strong as possible. Weapon storage is rarely an issue.

### 2 Average Robots

A balanced approach with two robots sharing the budget.

| Category | Amount | Details |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Facilities | ₡350,000 | Roster Expansion L1 + discount facilities |
| Robots | ₡1,000,000 | 2 robot frames |
| Weapons | ₡500,000 | 2–4 weapons across both robots |
| Attributes | ₡1,100,000 | Split between both robots |
| Reserve | ₡50,000 | Covers immediate repairs and operating costs |

Two robots means two battles per cycle, doubling your income potential and league experience. Roster Expansion Level 1 is mandatory before creating the second robot.

### 3 Flimsy Robots

Maximize battle participation with three robots, each starting weaker.

| Category | Amount | Details |
|----------|--------|---------|
| Facilities | ₡350,000 | Roster Expansion L2 + discount facilities |
| Robots | ₡1,500,000 | 3 robot frames |
| Weapons | ₡450,000 | 3–6 weapons, at least one per robot |
| Attributes | ₡650,000 | Spread thin across all three robots |
| Reserve | ₡50,000 | Covers immediate repairs and operating costs |

Three robots fighting means three income streams, but each robot starts weaker individually. Roster Expansion Level 2 is required for the third robot slot.

```callout-tip
The reserve of ₡50,000 is intentionally small. Your robots will start earning credits from battles immediately, so you don't need a large buffer. If you're cautious, you can shift some credits from attributes into reserve.
```

## Facility Purchase Order

The onboarding recommends buying facilities in this order:

1. **Roster Expansion** (mandatory for 2 or 3 robot strategies) — You can't create additional robots without it. Level 1 costs ₡150,000, Level 2 costs ₡300,000.
2. **Weapons Workshop** (₡75,000) — Buy before purchasing weapons to get 10% off every weapon.
3. **Training Facility** (₡150,000) — Buy before upgrading attributes to get 10% off every upgrade.
4. **Repair Bay** (₡50,000) — Reduces repair costs after battles. More valuable the more robots you run.

Optional facilities to consider with remaining budget:
- **Training Academies** (₡100,000 each) — Raise attribute caps beyond level 10. Pick the category that matches your build.
- **Merchandising Hub** (₡150,000) — Passive income that scales with prestige. Better as a mid-game investment.
- **Streaming Studio** (₡100,000) — Doubles streaming revenue per battle. More valuable with multiple robots.

## Early Game Tips

- **Buy discount facilities before the items they discount** — This is the single most impactful thing you can do with your starting budget.
- **Repair manually between cycles** — Using the Repair All button gives you a 50% discount on repairs compared to automatic cycle repairs. This adds up fast, especially with multiple robots. Manual repairs work even if your balance goes negative — it's the only action in the game that allows this.
- **Upgrade attributes in batches** — Focus on 6–8 key attributes rather than spreading thin across all 23.
- **Watch your cash flow** — After a few cycles, check your Financial Report to make sure income exceeds expenses.
- **Don't overbuy facilities** — Each facility has daily operating costs. Only buy what you'll actively benefit from right away.
