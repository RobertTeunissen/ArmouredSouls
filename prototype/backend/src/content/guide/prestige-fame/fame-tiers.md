## Overview

Every robot's [Fame](/guide/prestige-fame/fame-system) score is displayed alongside a **tier title** that reflects their individual reputation. There are six tiers, each representing a level of recognition in the Armoured Souls world.

![Fame tier progression](/images/guide/prestige-fame/fame-tier-progression.png)

## The Six Fame Tiers

| Tier | Fame Range | What It Represents |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Unknown** | 0 – 99 | A new or unremarkable robot. No audience, negligible streaming income. |
| **Known** | 100 – 499 | Starting to build a reputation. A small but growing fanbase. |
| **Famous** | 500 – 999 | A recognized competitor. Streaming income becomes noticeable. |
| **Renowned** | 1,000 – 2,499 | A well-known fighter with a dedicated following. Significant streaming revenue. |
| **Legendary** | 2,500 – 4,999 | One of the most celebrated robots in the game. Streaming income rivals battle winnings. |
| **Mythical** | 5,000+ | An all-time great. Maximum streaming audience and peak revenue generation. |

```callout-info
Fame tiers are displayed as badges next to your robot's name in battle results, leaderboards, and your roster. They're a visible indicator of your robot's individual legacy.
```

## Tier Progression

### Unknown (0 – 99)

Every robot starts here. At this tier, streaming income is negligible — nobody's watching an unknown fighter. Focus on winning battles to start building Fame.

### Known (100 – 499)

Your robot is starting to get noticed. A small fanbase is forming, and streaming income begins to trickle in. In Bronze league, reaching Known takes roughly 50 wins; in Gold, about 10 wins.

### Famous (500 – 999)

A recognized competitor with a real following. Streaming income becomes a noticeable part of your robot's contribution to the stable's finances. This is where the [Streaming Studio](/guide/facilities/facility-overview) facility starts paying for itself.

### Renowned (1,000 – 2,499)

A well-known fighter that draws crowds. Streaming revenue is now a significant income source, especially with a leveled-up Streaming Studio. At this tier, your robot's Fame is a genuine financial asset.

```callout-tip
Renowned is the sweet spot where Fame starts generating serious returns. If you have a robot approaching 1,000 Fame, make sure you have a Streaming Studio to capitalize on the audience growth.
```

### Legendary (2,500 – 4,999)

One of the most celebrated robots in the game. Streaming income at this tier can rival or exceed what the robot earns from battle winnings alone. Legendary robots are the stars of your stable.

### Mythical (5,000+)

An all-time great. Maximum streaming audience, peak revenue generation, and a permanent place in the game's history. Reaching Mythical requires hundreds of wins in upper-tier leagues or exceptional performance bonus consistency.

```callout-warning
Reaching Mythical takes serious commitment to a single robot. It's roughly 500+ Gold-tier wins or 125+ Champion-tier wins, not counting performance bonuses. Most stables will have one or two Legendary robots before any reach Mythical.
```

## Fame Tiers and Streaming Impact

Each tier represents a meaningful jump in streaming audience and revenue:

| Tier | Streaming Impact | Audience Size |
|------|-----------------|---------------|
| Unknown | Negligible | Almost no viewers |
| Known | Small | Growing audience |
| Famous | Moderate | Solid viewership |
| Renowned | Significant | Large, dedicated following |
| Legendary | Major | Massive audience |
| Mythical | Maximum | Peak viewership |

The relationship between Fame and streaming revenue is continuous (not stepped by tier), but the tier names give you a rough sense of where your robot stands in terms of audience appeal.

## Multiple Robots and Total Fame

Your stable's total streaming revenue depends on the combined Fame of all your robots. This means:

- **Multiple moderate-Fame robots** can generate more total streaming income than a single high-Fame robot
- **Each robot contributes independently** — a stable with three Famous robots (500 Fame each = 1,500 total) generates more streaming revenue than a stable with one Renowned robot (1,000 Fame)
- **Roster diversity pays off** — spreading battles across multiple robots builds Fame on each one

```callout-tip
Don't put all your eggs in one basket. Running 3–5 robots that each build Fame independently generates more total streaming revenue than focusing everything on a single star. The streaming formula rewards total stable Fame, not just your best robot.
```
