## Overview

**Fame** is your robot's individual reputation score. While [Prestige](/guide/prestige-fame/prestige-system) belongs to your stable as a whole, Fame is earned by each robot independently through their battle performances. A robot that wins consistently and impressively builds Fame faster than one that scrapes by with narrow victories.

Fame drives [streaming revenue](/guide/economy/streaming-revenue), creates individual robot identity, and rewards impressive battle performances with bonus earnings.

## How Robots Earn Fame

### Battle Victories

Every time a robot wins a battle, it earns Fame based on the league tier:

| League Tier | Fame per Win |
|-------------|-------------|
| Bronze | +2 |
| Silver | +5 |
| Gold | +10 |
| Platinum | +15 |
| Diamond | +25 |
| Champion | +40 |

Only the winning robot earns Fame. Draws and losses award nothing. Fame is tracked per-robot and persists permanently.

```callout-info
Fame values are lower than Prestige values because Fame is per-robot while Prestige is per-stable. A stable with 5 robots each earning Fame generates collective streaming revenue that can rival Prestige-based merchandising income.
```

### Performance Bonuses

Impressive victories earn bonus Fame on top of the base amount. These bonuses reward dominant play and clutch performances:

| Performance Type | Condition | Fame Multiplier |
|-----------------|-----------|----------------|
| **Perfect Victory** | Win without taking any HP damage | 2× (double Fame) |
| **Dominating Victory** | Win with more than 80% HP remaining | 1.5× (50% bonus) |
| **Comeback Victory** | Win after dropping below 20% HP | 1.25× (25% bonus) |

These multipliers apply to the base Fame earned for the league tier. For example, a Perfect Victory in Gold league earns 10 × 2 = 20 Fame instead of the base 10.

```callout-tip
Performance bonuses are the fastest way to build Fame. A robot that consistently wins with high HP (dominating victories) accumulates Fame 50% faster than one that wins by the skin of its teeth. Invest in defensive attributes and smart [yield thresholds](/guide/combat/yielding-and-repair-costs) to maximize your performance bonus rate.
```

### Streak Bonuses

Consecutive wins with the same robot trigger one-time streak bonuses:

| Streak Length | Bonus Fame |
|--------------|-----------|
| 5 consecutive wins | +10 |
| 10 consecutive wins | +25 |
| 25 consecutive wins | +50 |
| 50 consecutive wins | +100 |

Streak bonuses are awarded once per streak milestone. They reset when the robot loses a battle.

### Robot Milestones

Individual robots earn one-time Fame bonuses for reaching notable achievements:

- **ELO milestones** — Reaching ELO 1500, 1750, 2000, 2250, 2500
- **Battle count milestones** — 50, 100, 250, 500, 1000 battles fought
- **Kill count milestones** — 10, 25, 50, 100, 250 opponents reduced to 0 HP

These milestones create a sense of individual robot history and reward long-term investment in specific robots.

## What Fame Does

### Streaming Revenue

Fame is the primary driver of [streaming revenue](/guide/economy/streaming-revenue). Robots with higher Fame attract larger audiences to their battle broadcasts, generating more income per battle. Combined with the [Streaming Studio](/guide/facilities/facility-overview) facility, high-Fame robots become significant income generators.

### Robot Identity

Fame creates individual identity for your robots. A robot with high Fame is a "star" — recognized, respected, and valuable. Fame tiers provide visual badges and titles that distinguish your best performers.

### Tournament Performance

High-Fame robots attract attention in tournaments. While Fame doesn't affect matchmaking fairness (ELO handles that), famous robots may appear in featured matches and attract spectator interest.

```callout-warning
Fame is per-robot, not transferable. If you retire or lose a high-Fame robot, that Fame is gone. Protect your star robots — they're valuable assets that took many battles to build.
```

## Fame Growth Expectations

| Fame Target | Approximate Wins (Bronze) | Approximate Wins (Gold) |
|------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| 100 (Known) | ~50 wins | ~10 wins |
| 500 (Famous) | ~250 wins | ~50 wins |
| 1,000 (Renowned) | ~500 wins | ~100 wins |
| 2,500 (Legendary) | ~1,250 wins | ~250 wins |
| 5,000 (Mythical) | ~2,500 wins | ~500 wins |

These estimates assume standard victories without performance bonuses. Consistent dominating or perfect victories can cut these numbers significantly.
